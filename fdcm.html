Double Cross Method for Clock

Few years ago (2011), during a local gathering in my city, Bandung, we discuss a "method" to solve a Rubik's Clock. Usually the steps involved on solving clock are the following:

  1. Cross on Front Face, then align to 12.
  2. Flip the clock.
  3. Cross on Back Face
  4. Solve the corners
My friend, Fahmi Taufiqulhadi, proposed that probably we can do step 1 and 3 altogether. So the method will be:
  1. Cross on Front Face and Back Face, align to 12.
  2. Flip the clock.
  3. Solve the corners
He called that method "FDCM" (Fahmi Double Cross Method). Well, we don't know anyone who documented this method during that time. Personally, I consider this an "idea" and not yet a method.

Here is some demonstration on simulator by my friend, Cahyo Galuh Wicaksono, pretty decent attempt on his third day after learning clock:

Notice after he flips the clock, the cross on the backside is already solved.

There is a cuber I know who regularly use this method in official competition, Didiet Aditya Bayu Kusuma.

However, this method has drawbacks: A little math and memorization during inspection. But, I think one can be good at it with practice.

The Method

The easiest way to explain the method is by an example. Scramble the clock with:

		UUdd u=-2,d=-2 / dUdU  u=2,d=2 / ddUU u=-1,d=-5 / UdUd u=2,d=-3 /
		dUUU u=4 / UdUU u=6 / UUUd u=-5 / UUdU u=-3 / UUUU u=5 / dddd d=0 /
		dUdU

Your clock should be look like this

Front: 
		12  9  5
		 6  8  6
		11 10  8
Back: 
		 7  6 12
		10  8  5
		 4  3  1

1. Inspection Phase

Here is the drawbacks of this idea: doing math and memorization during inspection! And remember, we only have 15 seconds to do it. Thankfully, the math is simple, it's just 4 subtractions.

Now look at the Back Face, do the following:

  • Subtract center number (8) with top edge number (6) = 2
  • Subtract center number (8) with left edge number (10) = -2
  • Subtract center number (8) with bottom edge number (3) = 5
  • Subtract center number (8) with right edge number (5) = 3

[Notice it's counter clockwise starting from top edge]

So you need to memo: 2, -2, 5, 3

2. Double Cross Phase

Start from the Front Face

  • Position the pin as UUdd, align the center with bottom edge, to 10 [u=2]. Then do the first number that you memorized for d. [d=2]
  • Position the pin as dUdU, align the center with left edge, to 8 [u=-2]. Then do the second number that you memorized for d. [d=-2]
  • Position the pin as ddUU, align the center with top edge, to 9 [u=1]. Then do the third number that you memorized for d. [d=5]
  • Position the pin as UdUd, align the center with right edge, to 7 [u=-2]. Then do the third number that you memorized for d. [d=3]

[Notice that it's clockwise startying from bottom edge, also the pin position is also same as scrambling]

3. Solve Corners

Flip the clock, you should see cross on backside. Solve corners like usual.
